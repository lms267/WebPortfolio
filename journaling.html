Journaling Excercises

If you already regularly journal, then you are one step ahead! However, if journaling is not something that you regularly practice, there is never a bad time to start!

The first step is to get a journal. The world is your oyster: you could buy a regular old spiral notebook and a pen, you could use your iPad and Apple Pencil and keep a digital notebook, or you could buy a notebook with different journaling excercises already in them! If your journal doesn't have pre-prepared prompts, here are some ideas:

  • What are five things you want to accomplish by this time next year?
  • What is something you are currently saving up for, and why is it important to you?
  • What are your biggest strengths? How can you use them to achieve your goals?
  • What are your non-negotiables in life? In relationships? At work? At home?

These are just some examples, feel free to let your thoughts flow on their own! Once, you get into a flow, you can journal for hours, and it can be very freeing. Emotions can get bottled up very easily, so be sure to release them every now and then!
